A polynomial function is a function of the form

\( P(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + a_{n-2} x^{n-2} + \ldots + a_1 x + a_0,\)

\( \quad a_n \neq 0 \) where 'n' is a nonnegative integer,

\( a_0, a_1, \ldots, a_n \) are real numbers called coefficients,

\( a_n x^n \) is the leading term,

\( a_n \) is the leading coefficient,

and \( a_0 \) is the constant term.

A polynomial function is a type of mathematical equation that involves variables (usually represented by the letter 'x') raised to different powers and combined with coefficients (which are just numbers). These equations are written in a specific format, where terms with higher powers of 'x' come first and terms with lower powers come later.

For example, here's how a polynomial function looks:

\( P(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + a_{n-2} x^{n-2} + \ldots + a_1 x + a_0\)

In this equation:

  • P(x) represents the polynomial function.
  • x is the variable.
  • \( a_n, a_{n-1}, a_{n-2}, ... , a_1 x + a_0 \) are coefficients (which are just numbers) that multiply each term.
  • The powers of x (like \( x^n, x^{n-1}, x^{n-2}, ... ,x\)) determine the degree of the polynomial.

The highest power of 'x' in the equation is called the 'degree' of the polynomial.

\( P(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + a_{n-2} x^{n-2} + \ldots + a_1 x + a_0\)

For example, if the highest power is 3, we call it a 'third-degree polynomial'.

The coefficient of the term with the highest power of x (like an) is called the 'leading coefficient'.

P(x) = \( a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + a_{n-2} x^{n-2} + \ldots + a_1 x + a_0\)

Other notations:

f(x) = \( a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + a_{n-2} x^{n-2} + \ldots + a_1 x + a_0\)

y = \( a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + a_{n-2} x^{n-2} + \ldots + a_1 x + a_0\)
